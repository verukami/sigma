Verukami Work

Transforming University Digital Experience

Designing clarity, accessibility, and belonging across a complex academic ecosystem

Overview

The Blue School redesigned its website to better serve a diverse community including prospective students, faculty, alumni, press, and industry partners.

While the school had a strong conceptual and artistic identity, its digital experience had become difficult to navigate—especially for users outside the internal community.

I led the UX work to expand an initial homepage concept into a cohesive, accessible experience across the entire site.

The Challenge

  • Fragmented and overwhelming navigation
  • Lack of clear entry points for different user groups
  • Difficulty recognizing relevant content
  • Key information buried or hard to find

As a result, users often felt lost, unsure where they belonged, or how to complete even basic tasks.

My Role

Product / UX Designer

  • UX strategy
  • Information architecture
  • User flows and wireframes
  • Design system definition
  • UI design (per sprint)
  • Accessibility considerations

I worked closely with creative, development, and product stakeholders throughout the process.

Users & Needs

  • Prospective students — Inspiring, accessible program information
  • Faculty & staff — Fast access to internal resources
  • Press & media — News, events, official content
  • Industry partners — Collaboration and research opportunities
  • Alumni — Staying connected and informed

UX Approach

Audit & Research

I audited the existing site to evaluate content structure, navigation patterns, and accessibility gaps, supported by user feedback.

Information Architecture

I restructured the site architecture to create clear entry points by user type, reduce cognitive load, and improve content discoverability.

Wireframes & User Flows

Navigation paths and hierarchy were validated early through wireframes and flows, ensuring consistency across journeys.

Design System

I created a lightweight, scalable design system defining core components, typography, spacing, color usage, and accessibility standards.

It was designed to be Drupal-friendly, supporting smoother implementation and long-term scalability.

UI Design

UI templates were designed incrementally per sprint, focusing on editorial layouts and speaker profiles with strong hierarchy, consistency, and accessibility.

Outcome

  • Improved navigation clarity
  • Clearer content entry points
  • More accessible and scalable patterns
  • Stronger alignment between brand and usability

Reflection

This project reinforced the importance of early cross-team collaboration. While successful, earlier alignment could have strengthened execution.

Every decision remained focused on usability, accessibility, and long-term maintainability.
